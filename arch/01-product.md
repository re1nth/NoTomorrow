# 01 — Product Pillars

## Problem

College gives you scaffolding: syllabus, peers, deadlines, signals. Step out and
the scaffolding vanishes. Specialization is required to survive, but obsessing
over one dimension hollows out the others. Online courses train you for academic
excellence, not for shipping. There is no "next level" feedback loop the way
there is in a competitive game.

## Solution

A personal coach — modeled on Kamogawa from *Hajime no Ippo* — that:

- Understands your current level (via a competitive-coding-style rating)
- Generates a roadmap from where you are to a goal you actually care about
- Pushes you daily, demands proof, recalibrates as you improve or stall
- Treats you as a builder, not a student — every milestone ends in a shipped artifact

## Pillars (must survive every design decision)

1. **Builder over learner.** Every milestone ends in a shipped artifact (repo,
   deploy URL, demo video). Never a quiz.
2. **Adaptive, not prescribed.** Roadmaps regenerate as ratings move; no fixed
   curricula.
3. **Coach persona is the product.** The AI is Kamogawa-coded: intense, specific,
   calls you out. Voice and presence matter as much as correctness.
4. **Rating is earned, not claimed.** Elo-style updates only fire on verified
   proof.
5. **Bundles are user-owned.** Completed roadmaps become forkable templates. No
   centralized course catalog.
6. **Stay foolish.** Defaults bias toward ambitious horizons. The product should
   never talk a user down from an audacious goal — it should plan harder.

## Anti-goals

- Not a course platform. Not a Coursera/Udemy clone.
- Not a generic todo app dressed in anime art.
- Not a passive content library. If there's no shipping, there's no value.
